# Proposed AACSB Thought Leadership Narrative for Informatik (Karlstad Business School)

## Purpose
This document provides a structured interpretation of the repository's publication portfolio in relation to AACSB's notion of **thought leadership**: recognized authority, aligned with mission, creating impactful intellectual contributions for multiple stakeholder groups.

## What I reviewed
- 43 PDF publications in this repository (2020–2026), using filename patterns, embedded PDF metadata titles, and extractable text snippets where available.
- A generated inventory file (`publication_inventory.csv`) was used as the working index.

## Executive conclusion
A strong and defensible thought-leadership claim for Informatik is:

> **Human-centered digital transformation with societal impact**, particularly in (1) digital health data and patient-facing eHealth, (2) large-scale agile/software development capability, and (3) digitally enabled service innovation in public/education/mobility contexts.

These three streams are recurring over multiple years, involve non-academic stakeholders, and focus on usable, practice-relevant knowledge.

---

## Suggested Thought Leadership Domains

## 1) Trustworthy, patient-centered digital health and data governance (flagship domain)

**Observed evidence from publication titles/snippets**
- Encrypted medical data + ML stakeholder perspectives (2021).  
- Consent/engagement design and privacy notices on smartphones (2020, 2021).  
- Clinician and patient experiences with online records/EHR access (2021–2024).  
- Cross-country sociotechnical analyses and Nordic policy-facing perspective on patient online record access and European Health Data Space (2024).  
- Security/privacy perspectives among adolescents for online records (2024).

**Why this qualifies as AACSB-style thought leadership**
- **Recognized authority area**: portfolio shows continuity and specialization around eHealth data access, privacy, and human interaction.
- **Stakeholder breadth**: patients, clinicians, adolescents, policy actors, health system administrators, and cross-national partners.
- **Theory-practice bridge**: sociotechnical and affordance perspectives tied to practical design, access, and governance issues.
- **Societal value**: safer, more inclusive, and usable access to health information.

**How to frame this in accreditation language**
- "Informatik provides thought leadership on how to make digital health data access **trusted, usable, and equitable** across patient groups and national settings."

---

## 2) Large-scale agile development and software work-system sustainability

**Observed evidence from publication titles/snippets**
- Balancing stability/change in agile teams; ambidexterity in agile software development (2021).
- Team autonomy in SAFe contexts (2022).
- Technical/process/social debt as organizational and human challenges (2022–2025).
- Large-scale agile challenges and learning dynamics (2023–2025).

**Why this qualifies as AACSB-style thought leadership**
- **Clear focus over time**: recurring line of inquiry (agility at scale + debt dynamics + organization design).
- **Business relevance**: directly addresses productivity, coordination, employee well-being, and delivery risk in modern digital firms.
- **Actionable contributions**: concepts and evidence that managers can use in transformation programs.
- **Stakeholders**: industry teams, project leaders, digital transformation leaders, and software organizations.

**How to frame this in accreditation language**
- "Informatik contributes thought leadership on how organizations can scale agility while controlling technical, process, and social debt to sustain both performance and workforce outcomes."

## 3) Digital service innovation for inclusive societal systems (education, public sector, mobility)

**Observed evidence from publication titles/snippets**
- IoT for well-being in elementary schools and lessons from IoT experts (2023–2024).
- Open government data framework work (2022).
- Lifelong learning / higher-education design principles (2024).
- Multimodal air-rail journey integration and passenger-oriented service ecosystems (2025–2026).
- Social inclusion/poverty-related education work (2026).

**Why this qualifies as AACSB-style thought leadership**
- **Engagement orientation**: publications target real ecosystems with public and private actors.
- **Cross-sector impact**: schools, government data ecosystems, transport operators, and vulnerable populations.
- **Co-creation logic**: service ecosystem and stakeholder-based perspectives align with AACSB's impact/engagement framing.

**How to frame this in accreditation language**
- "Informatik advances thought leadership in digitally enabled service ecosystems that improve citizen, learner, and passenger outcomes through multi-actor collaboration."

## Recommended overarching narrative for the 2027 review

A concise umbrella statement:

> **Informatik at Karlstad Business School demonstrates thought leadership in human-centered digital transformation by producing societally engaged, practice-relevant research on trustworthy digital health, scalable agile organizing, and inclusive digital service ecosystems.**

This narrative is defensible because it demonstrates:
1. **Continuity** (multi-year streams).  
2. **Focus** (three coherent domains, not scattered topics).  
3. **Stakeholder engagement** (healthcare, industry, education, public sector, transport, society).  
4. **Impact potential** (policy relevance, managerial relevance, and improved user outcomes).

---

## Suggested evidence packaging for AACSB documentation
To make this persuasive during assessment, organize material as follows:

1. **Theme portfolios (3 folders/appendices)**
   - Each theme with key publications, methods used, external partners, and stakeholder outcomes.
2. **Impact cases (2–4 pages each)**
   - Example: patient online record access: what problem, what contribution, who used it, what changed.
3. **Stakeholder map**
   - Link each publication stream to students, business, healthcare, government, NGOs/society.
4. **Thought-leadership indicators**
   - Invitations, policy participation, practitioner talks, guideline influence, interdisciplinary collaborations.

## Notes on confidence and limits
- Several PDFs are difficult to text-extract cleanly due to encoding/compression, so this synthesis uses a mix of robust title metadata and partial content snippets.
- Confidence is **high** for the three-domain structure because the pattern appears repeatedly across years and authors.
